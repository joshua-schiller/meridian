# Interviewee Briefing

Use this when Bilguun plays Maya Chen in the live demo. The goal is not to recite a script. The goal is to make sure the transcript contains the evidence Meridian needs to produce a visibly sharper next AI interview plan.

## Persona

You are Maya Chen, product lead at Northstar Labs, a 22-person startup building workflow software for customer success teams. You run discovery without a dedicated researcher. You can get customers on calls, but the work after each call is where the process breaks down.

## Beats To Hit

Work these ideas into natural answers:

1. The hard part is not getting calls.
   - You can get customers on the phone.
   - The hard part is turning each call into useful follow-up research before the team has already moved on.

2. Broad questions last too long.
   - Your team keeps asking broad workflow questions even after the first call should have sharpened the hypothesis.
   - By call three or four, the guide should be more targeted than it usually is.

3. Context is scattered.
   - Sales notes live in CRM.
   - Support context is in Slack or tickets.
   - Product notes are in docs.
   - That context is rarely visible when preparing the next interview.

4. Trust requires evidence.
   - If an agent proposed the next question bank, you would need to see what changed, why it changed, and which quote or finding caused the change.
   - Confidence and direct quotes matter more than a generic score.

5. Reports need to support decisions.
   - Executives do not want raw transcripts.
   - They need the pattern, confidence level, representative quotes, and what decision the finding supports.

## Good Example Lines

- "The hard part is not getting calls. We can get customers on the phone."
- "We keep asking broad questions for too long."
- "Context is scattered. Sales notes live in the CRM, support has Slack threads, and product has docs."
- "If an agent proposes the next question bank, I need it to show me what changed, why it changed, and what evidence it came from."
- "Executives need the pattern, the confidence level, and a few quotes."

## Barge-In Moment

When Meridian starts a longer question, interrupt naturally:

"Sorry, before you finish that, the real issue is speed. We usually make the product decision before the synthesis comes back."

Then let Meridian recover and continue. The point is to show that Meridian is conducting the interview and can handle interruption gracefully.

## Stay In Bounds

- Do not mention that you are reading a script.
- Do not add unrelated features like email outreach, calendars, or recruiting.
- Do not make the pain only about scheduling; the product thesis is synthesis and adaptive follow-up.
- Keep answers substantive but short enough for a live demo.
